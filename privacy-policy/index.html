1. About This Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Automotive Insight ABN [INSERT ABN] ("we", "us", "our"), located at 206A Stubbs Terrace, Shenton Park WA 6008, collects, uses, discloses and stores your personal information.

By using our website at automotiveinsight.com.au or contacting us for services, you consent to the practices described in this policy.

2. What Personal Information We Collect

We collect personal information that is reasonably necessary to provide our automotive services and respond to enquiries. This may include:

  • Your name, email address and phone number
  • Your vehicle details (make, model, year, registration number)
  • Your suburb or general location (for service area purposes)
  • Information about the service or repair you require
  • Payment information (processed securely via third-party providers)
  • Technical information collected automatically when you visit our website (see Section 6)

3. How We Collect Personal Information

We collect personal information in the following ways:

  • Directly from you when you complete a booking enquiry form on our website
  • When you contact us by phone or email
  • When you visit our workshop in person
  • Through automated means when you browse our website (cookies, server logs)

We only collect personal information by lawful and fair means. We will not collect personal information that is unnecessary for our functions.

4. Why We Collect Personal Information

We use your personal information to:

  • Process and manage your vehicle service or repair booking
  • Contact you regarding your booking, job status or follow-up care
  • Provide you with service reminders or relevant updates (you may opt out at any time)
  • Comply with legal and regulatory obligations
  • Improve our website and services
  • Respond to feedback, complaints or enquiries

5. Disclosure of Personal Information

We do not sell, rent or trade your personal information to third parties. We may disclose your personal information to:

  • Parts suppliers or warranty providers where necessary to complete a repair
  • Our website hosting and IT service providers who assist us in operating our business
  • Government or regulatory bodies if required by law
  • Professional advisers (accountants, lawyers) where necessary and subject to confidentiality obligations

Any third parties we engage are required to handle your information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth).

6. Cookies and Website Analytics

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to improve your browsing experience and to understand how visitors use our site. Cookies are small text files stored on your device.

We may use analytics tools (such as Google Analytics) that collect anonymised data about website usage. This data does not personally identify you. You can disable cookies in your browser settings; however, some parts of our website may not function correctly as a result.

7. Storage and Security of Personal Information

We take reasonable steps to protect the personal information we hold from misuse, interference, loss, unauthorised access, modification or disclosure. These steps include password protection, encrypted transmission and restricted staff access.

Where personal information is no longer needed for any purpose for which it may be used or disclosed, and we are not required by law to retain it, we will take reasonable steps to destroy or de-identify it.

8. Access and Correction

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you, and to request correction of information that is inaccurate, out of date, incomplete or misleading.

To make a request, contact us using the details in Section 11. We will respond within a reasonable timeframe (generally within 30 days). We may need to verify your identity before processing a request.

In some circumstances, we may be unable to provide access (for example, where doing so would unreasonably impact the privacy of another individual). We will advise you in writing if this is the case.

9. Complaints

If you believe we have not handled your personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), you may lodge a complaint with us by contacting us as set out in Section 11. We will investigate your complaint and respond within 30 days.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may escalate your complaint to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC):
